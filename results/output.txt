### Diagnosis: the Double-Correction trap
TabPFN OOF collapsed from N14v3's raw 0.94872 (balance_probabilities=True only) to 0.93302
(balance_probabilities=True + manual `proba /= class_priors`) — a -0.0157 regression. This is
the exact trap logged at N07 ("Double Correction"): `balance_probabilities=True` already solves
the log-loss vs balanced-accuracy divergence internally; a second manual division over-corrects
toward minority classes and destroys calibration. The fusion Nelder-Mead search correctly detected
this and assigned TabPFN a blend weight of 0.0 — TabPFN contributed nothing to the L2 Meta result.
Net effect: prior-correction is a regression, not an improvement, whenever the base model already
applies `balance_probabilities=True` (or an equivalent internal correction). See Rules.md Rule 21.

### RealMLP verdict
0.94797 OOF, below GBDT's 0.94996, at ~5.8h/5-fold cost (20907s in this run). Dropped from N17
and future notebooks — the diversity did not translate into a solo or blend win worth the compute.

## SOTA research sweep (2026-07-20)
Exhaustive search across internal priors + web (TabArena NeurIPS 2025, TabICL ICML 2025/v2 2026,
Chunked TabPFN arXiv 2509.00326, multiclass threshold-tuning arXiv 2511.21794, S6E7 community
writeups) to find any viable organic lever beyond what's been tried:
- TabArena (arXiv 2506.16791): GBDT-vs-DL is a false dichotomy; peak performance requires
  post-hoc ensembling across model families. TabM ranks #1 after post-hoc ensembling (above
  LightGBM, RealMLP, CatBoost). Validates our whole cross-family blend architecture.
- TabICLv2 (soda-inria/tabicl, pip-installable, sklearn API): column-then-row attention,
  handles 500K+ rows via CPU/disk offloading, beats TabPFNv2 AND CatBoost on >10K-row datasets.
  This removes TabPFN's core handicap (100K subsample = only 14.5% of a 552K fold-train set).
- TabM (yandex-research/tabm, pip-installable, pure PyTorch): parameter-efficient ensemble of
  k=32 MLPs trained in parallel, satisfies Rule 10 (PyTorch-only for DL).
- Multiclass threshold-tuning literature (GHOST, arXiv 2511.21794) confirms our Lever 2 is
  state-of-practice; nothing structurally stronger exists for post-hoc calibration.
- Community: a blog covering this exact S6E7 competition (Lukyanenko/Antigravity) peaked at
  LB 0.94886 -- we are well ahead of published public approaches.
Verdict: TabICLv2 and TabM are the two strongest untried orthogonal information sources.
Built into N18 as guarded families.
